How to Get a Business Loan
with Bad Credit in 2026

Banks approve fewer than 20% of small business loan applications — and if your personal credit score is below 680, that number drops even further. But here's what the banks don't tell you: credit score is one data point, not the whole story. Lenders who understand small business look at a much fuller picture.

This guide covers exactly what alternative lenders evaluate, which products are available at each credit tier, and what you can do today to improve your funding odds — even before your score improves.

Why Credit Score Isn't Everything

Traditional banks built their underwriting models for large, established businesses with long credit histories, ample collateral, and years of audited financials. Small businesses — especially those under three years old — don't fit that mold, and their credit scores often don't reflect their actual ability to repay.

Alternative lenders and fintech platforms shifted this model by looking at:

  • Monthly revenue — consistent cash flow is a stronger repayment signal than a credit score
  • Average daily balance — shows whether the business can handle a daily or weekly payment
  • Revenue trends — a business growing 20% year-over-year is a better bet than a flat business with a 720 score
  • Industry and time in business — certain industries have lower default rates regardless of credit profile
  • Liens and bankruptcies — a resolved bankruptcy is weighed differently than an open judgment

Business Loan Options by Credit Score

Credit score 680+ — Traditional and SBA options open up

At 680 and above, you qualify for most alternative lending products and some traditional bank products. SBA loans require at least 650 in practice, and most conventional banks want 680–720. Term loans in the $50K–$500K range are accessible, with rates that reflect your stronger profile.

Credit score 600–679 — Alternative lending sweet spot

Most online term loans and equipment financing programs are available at this tier. Expect slightly higher rates than prime-credit borrowers, but a wide range of products. Equipment financing is particularly accessible here because the equipment itself serves as collateral, reducing the lender's risk.

Credit score 550–599 — Revenue-based products

At this range, the emphasis shifts fully to business performance. Merchant cash advances and short-term business loans are available if your monthly revenue is $10,000+. Invoice factoring is also an option if you have outstanding receivables — your client's creditworthiness matters more than yours.

Credit score 500–549 — Still options exist

It's harder, but not impossible. MCAs and revenue-based loans are available to businesses with strong consistent deposits. You may also qualify for purchase order financing if you have confirmed purchase orders from creditworthy buyers. Expect shorter terms, lower advance rates, and higher factor rates.

Below 500 — Focus on building before borrowing

Below 500, the cost of capital gets high enough that borrowing can hurt more than it helps. At this point, it's usually worth a 90-day credit repair effort before applying. What Lenders Actually Review

When you apply through a network like Guidance Funding, underwriters across 75+ lenders review your application simultaneously. Here's what they're looking at:

  • 3 months of business bank statements — the primary data source for alternative lenders
  • Average monthly deposits — a rough revenue figure underwriters use to size your offer
  • NSF (non-sufficient funds) frequency — more than 3–4 NSFs per month is a red flag
  • Negative days — how often the account balance went below zero
  • Credit card processing statements (if applicable) — used for MCA sizing
  • Business age — most lenders require 6–12 months in business
  • Outstanding loans or advances — stacking (taking multiple advances simultaneously) is heavily scrutinized

5 Ways to Improve Your Funding Odds Before You Apply

1. Clean up your bank statements

The 30–90 days before you apply matter most. Keep your average daily balance positive, reduce NSFs to zero, and don't let the account run near zero. This single factor can move you from a decline to an approval faster than any credit score improvement.

2. Pay down revolving credit balances

Credit utilization — how much of your available credit you're using — accounts for 30% of your FICO score. Paying down revolving balances below 30% utilization can add 20–50 points in 30 days.

3. Dispute inaccurate items

Roughly 1 in 5 credit reports contain errors that negatively affect scores. Request your free reports from AnnualCreditReport.com and dispute anything inaccurate. Corrections take 30–45 days to reflect.

4. Don't apply everywhere at once

Multiple hard inquiries in a short period signal desperation to underwriters and reduce your score. Working with a broker like Guidance Funding means one soft inquiry triggers matches across 75+ lenders — not 75 hard pulls.

5. Show revenue growth

If your deposits have been trending up month-over-month, make sure that's visible in your statements before you apply. Consistent growth tells a better story than a static high balance.

Products Built for Low-Credit Business Owners

Not all business funding products are created equal when it comes to credit requirements. These products are specifically designed to work for businesses with challenged credit:

  • Merchant Cash Advance — No fixed credit minimum; based on monthly card volume or bank deposits. Fast funding in 24–48 hours.
  • Invoice Factoring — Your clients' credit matters, not yours. Advance rates of 70–90% on outstanding invoices.
  • Purchase Order Financing — Fund confirmed orders before you produce or deliver. Creditworthiness of the buyer is the primary factor.
  • Equipment Financing — Equipment serves as collateral, reducing the lender's risk and the credit score bar.
  • Credit Education — Not a loan, but a guided program to build business and personal credit for better rates in 90–180 days.

The Bottom Line

Bad credit doesn't close the door on business funding — it just changes which door you walk through.
