Merchant Cash Advance

A merchant cash advance (MCA) provides fast capital against your future business revenue. Instead of fixed monthly payments, you repay through a percentage of daily or weekly sales — which means payments adjust with your cash flow.

How Merchant Cash Advance works

You receive a lump sum upfront. Repayment is made automatically as a fixed percentage of your daily credit card sales (or through fixed daily/weekly ACH withdrawals). When sales are strong, you pay more; when sales slow, you pay less. The advance is paid off when the agreed total is reached.

Who it's best for

  • Businesses with strong daily credit card sales
  • Bridging short-term cash flow gaps
  • Seasonal businesses with fluctuating revenue
  • Companies that can't qualify for traditional loans
  • Quick capital needs (inventory, payroll, opportunities)

Requirements

  • Minimum 3 months in business
  • Monthly revenue of $10,000+
  • Business bank account with consistent deposits
  • No minimum credit score required for many programs

Advantages

  • Fast approval and funding — often within 24 hours
  • Works with bad credit and prior bankruptcies
  • Flexible repayment adjusts with sales
  • No collateral required
  • Minimal documentation

Frequently asked questions

An MCA is a purchase of your future receivables, not a loan. Repayment is tied to sales performance rather than a fixed monthly payment. This means it's more flexible with cash flow but typically has a higher overall cost than traditional loans.

MCAs have the lowest credit requirements of any business funding product. Many of our MCA programs approve businesses with credit scores as low as 500 — some programs don't require a minimum score at all.

A factor rate is the total cost of the advance, expressed as a decimal (e.g., 1.25 means you'll repay $125,000 on a $100,000 advance). Factor rates are different from APRs and are not based on time to repay.
