Home Equity Line of Credit

A Home Equity Line of Credit lets you borrow against the equity you've built in your home — but unlike a lump-sum loan, you draw funds as needed and pay interest only on what you actually use. It's one of the cheapest, most flexible sources of capital available to business owners who own real estate.

How HELOC works

After an application and home appraisal, you're approved for a credit line — typically up to 80% combined loan-to-value (including your first mortgage). During the draw period (usually 10 years) you can pull funds as needed via checks, online transfer, or a linked card. You pay interest only on the balance drawn, not the full line. After the draw period ends, the line enters repayment (typically 10-20 years) where you pay principal + interest. Rates are variable, tied to Prime.

Who it's best for

  • Homeowners with 20%+ equity looking for flexible capital
  • Business owners who want low-cost funds without tapping retirement
  • Cases where timing of capital needs is uncertain (draw as needed)
  • Debt consolidation at a lower blended rate
  • Funding home improvements, tuition, or business expansion

Requirements

  • Primary residence, second home, or investment property ownership
  • At least 15-20% equity in the property (higher for investment properties)
  • Credit score of 660+ (720+ for the best rates)
  • Debt-to-income ratio below 43%
  • Documented income (W-2, tax returns, or business financials)
  • Recent home appraisal (we arrange)

Advantages

  • Only pay interest on what you actually draw
  • Rates typically much lower than business loans or credit cards
  • Funds available for 10 years without reapplying
  • Potential tax advantages (consult your CPA)
  • Ongoing access to capital as needs evolve
  • No prepayment penalties on most programs

Frequently asked questions

A home equity loan is a lump sum at a fixed rate, repaid like a term loan. A HELOC is a revolving line of credit — draw as needed, repay, draw again. HELOCs are better when you want flexibility or aren't sure exactly how much you'll need; fixed loans are better when you need a specific amount right away at a locked-in rate.

HELOC rates are almost always variable, calculated as Prime Rate + a margin based on your credit profile and LTV. Some lenders offer fixed-rate conversion on portions of your balance. Your rate will adjust over the life of the line as Prime moves.

The line converts to a repayment period (typically 10-20 years) where you can no longer draw new funds and must pay down principal + interest. Some lenders offer balloon options; most amortize fully. Your specialist will walk through the specific terms of any offer.
