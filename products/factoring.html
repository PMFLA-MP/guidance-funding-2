Invoice Factoring

Invoice factoring lets you sell your unpaid customer invoices to a factoring company for immediate cash — typically 80-95% of the invoice value upfront, with the remainder (minus a small fee) paid when your customer settles. It's not a loan; it's a cash flow tool that converts receivables into working capital.

How Invoice Factoring works

After a quick setup, you submit invoices to Guidance Funding's factoring partner. They verify the invoice and advance you 80–95% of the value within 24 hours. When your customer pays (directly to the factor), you receive the remaining balance minus the factoring fee. It's a revolving facility — use it as much or as little as you need.

Who it's best for

  • B2B businesses with net-30, 60, or 90 payment terms
  • Trucking and freight companies
  • Staffing agencies and professional services
  • Manufacturing and distribution
  • Government and commercial contractors
  • Businesses with customers who pay slowly

Requirements

  • B2B or B2G business (not direct-to-consumer)
  • Invoices to creditworthy commercial customers
  • Minimum 3 months in business
  • Monthly invoicing of $10,000+
  • Your customers' credit matters more than yours

Advantages

  • No debt added to your balance sheet
  • Approval based on your customers' credit, not yours
  • Scales automatically with revenue
  • Unlock cash from slow-paying customers
  • No long-term commitment required
