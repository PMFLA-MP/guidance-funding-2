SBA Loans for Small Business

SBA loans are small business loans partially guaranteed by the U.S. Small Business Administration. Because the government assumes part of the risk, lenders can offer longer repayment terms and lower rates than conventional business financing. They're the gold standard for established businesses seeking substantial, affordable capital.

How SBA Loans works

You apply through a Guidance Funding specialist who matches you to SBA-preferred lenders in our network. The SBA guarantees a portion of your loan, allowing lenders to offer favorable terms. The process takes longer than alternative financing — typically 30 to 90 days — but the rates and terms are significantly better.

Who it's best for

  • Established businesses needing large amounts of capital
  • Commercial real estate purchase or refinance (SBA 504)
  • Working capital and business acquisition (SBA 7(a))
  • Quick, smaller SBA needs under $500K (SBA Express)
  • Businesses wanting the lowest available rates

Requirements

  • Minimum 2 years in business (strong preference)
  • Credit score 680+ recommended
  • Strong cash flow and profitability
  • U.S.-based for-profit business
  • Tax returns (personal and business, last 2–3 years)
  • Detailed financial statements and business plan

Advantages

  • Lowest rates available for business financing
  • Longest repayment terms (up to 25 years)
  • Large loan amounts up to $5 million
  • Builds strong business credit
  • Multiple program types for different needs

Frequently asked questions

SBA loans typically take 30 to 90 days from application to funding, depending on the program and lender. SBA Express loans can close in as little as 30 days. For faster capital needs, we also offer bridge financing while your SBA loan is in process.

SBA 7(a) loans are the most flexible — used for working capital, inventory, business acquisition, or real estate up to $5M. SBA 504 loans are specifically for major fixed assets like commercial real estate and large equipment, structured with a lender + CDC partnership.

SBA loans over $25,000 generally require collateral to the extent available, but lack of collateral alone isn't a disqualifier. The SBA guarantee reduces lender risk, so approvals can happen even when collateral is limited.
