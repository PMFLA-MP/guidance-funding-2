Business Term Loans

A business term loan gives you a lump sum of capital that's repaid over a fixed schedule with predictable payments. It's the most straightforward form of business financing — ideal for planned investments, expansion, hiring, equipment purchases, or consolidating higher-cost debt.

How Term Loans Work

You receive the full loan amount upfront in a lump sum. Repayment is made on a fixed schedule — typically weekly or monthly — with a consistent payment amount for the life of the loan. Once paid off, many borrowers qualify for renewal with improved terms.

Who it's best for

  • Planned business investments and expansion
  • Hiring, opening a new location, or renovations
  • Consolidating higher-cost business debt
  • Predictable cash flow planning

Requirements

  • Minimum 6 months in business (1+ years preferred for best rates)
  • Monthly revenue of $10,000 or more
  • Credit score of 600+ (lower scores considered for certain products)
  • Business bank statements (last 3 months)
  • Government-issued ID

Advantages

  • Fixed, predictable monthly payments
  • Larger amounts available than short-term products
  • Build business credit with on-time payments
  • No collateral required for most borrowers
  • Renewal options after 40%+ paydown

Frequently asked questions

Guidance Funding term loans range from 12 months to 5 years depending on the loan amount and your business profile. Shorter terms mean higher payments but less total interest; longer terms reduce monthly payments.

A term loan gives you a lump sum upfront with a fixed repayment schedule. A line of credit is revolving — you draw funds as needed, pay interest only on what you use, and your credit replenishes as you repay.
